Global entities, fund managers keen to invest in India: Srinivas

By PSU Watch Bureau     November 10, 2022

Global entities, fund managers are keen to invest in India but need a platform that intermediates global capital flows into the domestic economy, said Injeti Srinivas, Chairman, IFSCA

Global entities, fund managers keen to invest in India: Srinivas

New York: Global entities and fund managers are keen to invest in India, said Injeti Srinivas, Chairman of the International Financial Services Centres Authority (IFSCA). He also underlined the need to create a platform that intermediates global capital flows into the domestic economy. Srinivas is leading a delegation of senior officials from the International Financial Services Centre (IFSC) at GIFT City (Gujarat International Finance Tec-City), who are currently in the US for meetings and roundtable discussions with American and global investors, banking and educational institutions for business opportunities. The delegation is on a visit to New York and Washington.

Fund managers keen to invest in India: Srinivas

“We had meetings with fund managers. They are all very keen to invest in India,” Srinivas said on October 9 in an exclusive interview. He said that the groups are happy to learn about various facilities available, apart from the regulatory comfort and overall financial ecosystem at GIFT. “They were excited about the cooperation prospects. India is a sought-after destination,” he added.

Create platform where we can easily intermediate global capital flows into domestic economy: Srinivas

“Most corporate boards today discuss India. It’s an opportunity for India and we need to reach out, connect and create a platform where we can easily intermediate global capital flows into the domestic economy. It’s a new India, it’s powered with technology, it’s very aspirational. There is a lot of efficiency, whether it is start-ups or in the fintech areas,” Srinivas said, adding that India is getting into the global supply chain and cited the example of Apple phones and semiconductors. “We want this to happen in the financial sector as well and that’s where GIFT fits into the equation,” he added.

Delegation visits NYSE to identify areas for mutual cooperation

“The delegation visited the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) and had discussions with President Lynn Martin and Vice Chairman John Tuttle, and identified areas for mutual cooperation. They also met Vice Chairman of leading investment management company BlackRock Robert Fairbairn as well as Managing Director at Cerberus Capital Management Kent Heggerud to discuss business opportunities at GIFT IFSC,” said the statement by IFSCA.

Representatives from 20 foreign institutions join roundtable interactions at CGI, NY

“Several roundtable interactions were organised focusing on opportunities at GIFT IFSC for global stakeholders, including with High Net-worth Individuals from the diaspora community, a roundtable interaction on the theme, ‘Aircraft leasing and financing.’ The delegation made a presentation on business opportunities at IFSC in the areas of aircraft leasing and financing business. IFSCA had also organised a roundtable interaction with universities and educational institutions on the establishment of foreign educational institutions/universities in GIFT IFSC. Representatives from more than 20 foreign universities have participated in the roundtable held at the Consulate General of India in New York,” said the statement by IFSCA.
